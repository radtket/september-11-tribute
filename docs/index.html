Answering the Call

The terrorist attacks on September 11th, 2001 transformed America's approach to homeland security. US Customs Service and Immigration and Naturalization Service personnel provided tremendous assistance in Ground Zero recovery efforts and efforts to secure the border following the attacks. More than four hundred agents took temporary duty assignments in New York City and hundreds of other agents went to airports and land ports of entry to provide security and search vehicles entering the country. These agents embody the HSI mission of protecting the homeland with honor, service, and integrity. HSI is grateful for their dedication and sacrifice.

On September 11th, 2001, two hijacked planes crashed into the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center, a third one crashed into the Pentagon, and a fourth plane crashed in rural Pennsylvania. A US Customs office was located in building 6 of the World Trade Center complex. It was destroyed when the North Tower collapsed. Customs Special Agents entered the gutted building and stood guard over a vault protecting sensitive information. Outside of Ground Zero, hundreds of Agents from the Immigration and Naturalization Service joined Customs Special Agents to secure airports and land entry points, providing security at airports and inspecting vehicles.

In the weeks and months that followed, US Customs personnel worked around the clock to sort through World Trade Center wreckage at the Fresh Kills Facility in Staten Island. They combed through thousands of tons of debris looking for evidence, weapons, and human remains. Additionally, nearly 1,000 Immigration and Naturalization Service Special Agents supported criminal investigations across the country related to the Pentagon, Twin Towers, and Shanksville, PA attacks.  

September 11th changed America's approach to homeland security. In 2003, the Department of Homeland Security was created, bringing together 22 federal departments and agencies. The investigative functions of the US Customs Service and Immigration and Naturalization Service joined forces to create Homeland Security Investigations (HSI), part of the newly created Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).

HSI strives to embody the spirit of honor, service, and integrity demonstrated by the US Customs and Immigration and Naturalization Service personnel who helped the nation recover following the worst ever terrorist attack on American soil.

Timeline of Events

A chronological order of events and highlights from September 2001 through November 2002.
09.11.01 09.11.01 09.19.01 09.20.01 09.21.01 10.08.01 11.04.01 11.05.01 02.26.02 04.23.02 11.25.02
September 11, 2001

America is Attacked

Nineteen Al-Qaeda militants hijacked four planes, killing almost 3,000 people and triggering an evolution of America's approach to border security. Two planes were flown into the twin towers of the World Trade Center in New York City. The third plane was flow into the Pentagon. The final plane, Flight 93, crashed in a Pennsylvania field during an attempt by flight crew and passengers to regain control of the plane.

September 11, 2001

Evacuation from WTC 6

New York Field Office Director John Martuge leads the safe evacuation of 800 Customs employees from the US Customhouse located at WTC 6.

September 19, 2001

Terrorism Response Task Force Created

The US Customs Service creates the Terrorism Response Task Force to coordinate all investigative and intelligence leads related to the September 11th attacks. One hundred and twenty-five Customs Agents begin traveling on behalf of the Federal Aviation Administration on commercial flights until a sufficient number of air marshals were trained.

September 20, 2001

The Immigration and Naturalization Service Joins the Investigation

Nearly 1,000 INS Special Agents join the criminal investigation of the Pentagon, Twin Towers, and Shanksville, PA attacks.

September 21, 2001

Customs Agents Arrive at Ground Zero

Agents from field offices across the United States arrive in New York City to assist with rescue and recovery efforts and search for human remains, evidence, and sensitive information in government buildings.

October 8, 2001

Office of Homeland Security Created

President George W. Bush signs Executive Order 13228, establishing the Office of Homeland Security and the Homeland Security Council. Its mission is to "develop and coordinate the implementation of a comprehensive national strategy to secure the United States from terrorist threats and attacks."

November 4, 2001

Customs Agents Arrive at Fresh Kills

The Fresh Kills landfill, named for "fresh water" in Dutch, is used as a sorting location for Ground Zero evidence. Customs agents comb through thousands of pounds of wreckage, looking for evidence. More than one million tons of materials from the World Trade Center is sorted at Fresh Kills.

November 5, 2001

Ground Zero Evidence Tested for Hazardous Materials

The Department of Health and Human Services issues a memorandum addressing concerns that materials collected from Ground Zero are contaminated. US Customs survey evidence stored at the US Customs Service Evidence Vault in New Jersey and find that materials from Ground Zero contain up to 25% chrysotile asbestos.

February 26, 2002

Agents Report Back to Regular Duty

Agents serving at recovery sites across New York and border entry points across the country return to regular duty.

April 23, 2002

Congress Thanks Customs Agents

The House of Representatives issues a recognition of the men and women of the US Customs Service, 6 World Trade Center Office for their hard work, commitment, and compassion during and immediately after the attacks.

November 25, 2002

Department of Homeland Security Created

The Homeland Security Act of 2002 creates the Department of Homeland Security, bringing together 22 federal departments and agencies in an effort to unify homeland security efforts.
